The full pitch

"...if Haiti could be kept free from revolutions, she might easily become, in proportion to her territory and population, the richest country in the world..." — Frederick Douglass, First American Minister to Haiti

What do Singapore, China, South Korea, and Botswana have in common?

They all went through a major transformation — starting extremely poor and becoming wealthy nations.

China is the clearest example. Adam Smith described 18th-century China like this:

"The poverty of the lower ranks of people in China far surpasses that of the most beggarly nations in Europe…"

That was China then. This is China now.

Today China is the second wealthiest nation in the world. Here is what changed for some nations that pulled ahead:

China: $76 GDP per capita (1961) → $12,597 (2023)
Singapore: $425 (1961) → $84,734+ (2023)
South Korea: $158 (1961) → $35,563+ (2023)
Botswana: $63 (1961) → $7,249+ (2023)

How did they do it?

They adopted capitalism and allocated capital properly. Mangé Lakay applies that same discipline — starting with food, reinvesting profits, and building over a 40-year horizon.

Why Mangé Lakay — and why now

With Mangé Lakay ("home food"), we are building local food supply and long-term value for the people of Kotfè — my hometown.

Right now Kotfè's economy is in dire condition. It has regressed since the 1970s.

Haiti: $68 GDP per capita (1961) → $1,693 (2023)
  • Botswana started $5 poorer than Haiti — now ~4× richer.
  • Singapore was ~6× richer than Haiti in 1961 — now ~50× richer.

The canal my grandfather helped build in the 1950s is still the only water system for farmers — and it is poorly maintained. Our city is moving backward. Donations help us move forward.

Why Kotfè?

  • Most citizens spend 90% of income on food
  • Many eat only 4 days per week on average
  • Many walk miles daily to fetch water
  • Lower security risk than the capital
  • It is my hometown — my full family lineage is here
  • Haiti is a free-market nation with enormous untapped opportunity

What we are building toward

This is a 40-year vision, not an overnight fix. Donations fund the steps along the way.

  • Local food security for Kotfè's 50,000+ residents
  • 150,000 eggs per day at full scale — affordable protein for the region
  • Reinvested agriculture → development → transportation → infrastructure
  • Quarterly reports so supporters see exactly where money went

Our model: start with profitable agriculture, amass capital from operations and donations, and allocate into capital-intensive industries over time.

40-year roadmap

Decade 1 — Food

150,000 eggs/day · corn surplus · vetiver for biofuel and erosion control · scale toward 200,000 people fed daily.

Decade 2 — Development

Buildings, schools, churches, canals, farming consulting, and value-added food products.

Decade 3 — Transportation

Taxis, tap-taps, buses, tourist transport, and distribution networks.

Decade 4 — Infrastructure

Roads, airports, ports, canals, water systems, and energy.

What we have done — and why we exist

We started selling boiled eggs — a popular breakfast in Haiti. With egg cost and labor, we earned a 30% profit margin.

Then we hit a wall: we ran out of eggs to buy.

At Walmart I can buy 40+ cartons of 60 eggs in one day. In Kotfè (50,000+ people), the market could not supply more than 100 eggs.

That scarcity is why Mangé Lakay exists: we must supply the market ourselves.
