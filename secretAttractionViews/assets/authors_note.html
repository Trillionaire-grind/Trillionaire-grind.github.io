Author's Note

The story behind A Secret Attraction Between Mentor and Mentee

This story started with a question I couldn't shake: What happens when two ambitious people feel something they're not supposed to feel — and every professional boundary tells them to look away?

LaShana came to me first. I wanted a heroine rooted in legacy — not inherited wealth for its own sake, but a family rhythm of hard work, hospitality, and showing up every day. Hotels felt right: they're places where strangers become guests, where presentation matters, and where one bad night can undo years of trust. When LaShana builds her own hotel, she's not just starting a business. She's carrying forward everything her grandparents and parents stood for.

Michael arrived as her opposite and her mirror — successful, visible, leading a hot tech company, and genuinely invested in helping other entrepreneurs grow. The mentor–mentee frame wasn't a gimmick. It's one of the most charged dynamics in real life: admiration, vulnerability, power, and proximity, all in the same room.

I wrote this for anyone who has ever told themselves it's just respect or it's just chemistry in a high-pressure season — and then realized it was something deeper. LaShana and Michael aren't reckless people looking for drama. They're builders. That's what makes the attraction harder to fight, and harder to ignore.

Thank you for reading — and for starting with the first chapter. I hope you feel the tension from page one, and I hope you root for both of them even when the story asks impossible things of them.
