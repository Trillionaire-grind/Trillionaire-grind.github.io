SECRET #2

"Master The Basics Of Your Cell Phone"

Your phone has hundreds of features. You need about ten of them. This lesson covers the ten — as simple recipes you can practice one at a time.

1. The escape hatch (learn this first)

From any screen: on an iPhone, swipe up from the very bottom edge (or press the round button). On Android, tap the circle at the bottom. You are now home, safe, every time. Lost? Go home and start again. This is the move that makes everything else fearless.

2. Making a call

Tap the green Phone icon → tap Keypad → dial → tap the green button. Better: tap Contacts, tap the person's name, tap the number. No dialing, no wrong numbers.

3. Favorites — your speed dial

In the Phone app, open a family member's contact card and tap Add to Favorites (iPhone) or the star (Android). Now your five most-called people are two taps away.

4. Texting — and the microphone trick

Open Messages, tap the person, tap the typing box. Now the secret weapon: tap the microphone button and just talk. Your phone types every word for you. Most seniors who "can't text" simply never learned that typing is optional.

5. Sending a photo to family

Open Photos → tap the picture → tap the share button (a square with an arrow pointing up) → tap the person's name → send. Practice with one photo today; by the third time it will feel natural.

6. The camera

Open Camera, point, tap the big round button. To see the picture you just took, tap the small square in the corner. Take ten pictures right now — they cost nothing and you can delete any of them (tap the trash can).

7. Make everything bigger

This is the setting that changes daily life. Open Settings → Display & Brightness (iPhone) or Display (Android) → Text Size — drag the slider until reading is effortless. While you're there, turn the brightness up. Squinting is not a requirement of owning a phone.

8. Volume and the flashlight

The buttons on the side of the phone are volume — up when a call is quiet, down in church. The flashlight lives in the quick menu: swipe down from the top-right corner (iPhone) or from the top (Android) and tap the flashlight icon. No more fumbling for light switches at night.

9. Charging without ruining the battery

Charge it every night while you sleep, like putting the car in the garage. You cannot "overcharge" a modern phone — it stops by itself. A phone that dies at 4pm is a phone that wasn't charged last night, nothing more.

10. Your daily recipe card

This week, practice one recipe per day: Monday — escape hatch ×10. Tuesday — call a favorite. Wednesday — dictate a text. Thursday — send a photo. Friday — make the text bigger. Fifteen minutes each. By Sunday your phone will feel like yours.
