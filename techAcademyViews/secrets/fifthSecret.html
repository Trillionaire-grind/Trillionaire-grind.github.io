SECRET #5

"5 Things You Need To Know About A.I."

You've heard the news stories — A.I. is going to change everything, take over everything, or ruin everything, depending on the channel. Here are the five things that are actually true, and actually useful, for you.

1. A.I. is a prediction machine, not a mind

Artificial intelligence is a computer program that read almost everything ever written and got extremely good at predicting what words come next. That's the whole trick. It isn't alive, it doesn't want anything, and it isn't watching you. Think of it as the world's most well-read parrot — astonishingly useful, occasionally wrong, never magical.

2. You can talk to it like a person — and it never gets impatient

Tools like ChatGPT let you ask questions in plain English and get plain-English answers. No menus, no jargon, no "hold for the next available representative." Ask it anything: "Explain Medicare Part D like I'm new to it." "Write a birthday note for my granddaughter who loves horses." "What can I cook with chicken, rice, and a bell pepper?"

Here's the part made for seniors: A.I. is the patient teacher you always wanted. It will explain the same thing ten times, ten different ways, and never sigh. Ask it the "embarrassing" tech questions you'd never ask your son-in-law.

3. It is confidently wrong sometimes

A.I. sounds certain even when it's mistaken — like a smooth talker at a dinner party. For recipes, letters, and explanations, that's harmless. For medical, legal, or money decisions, treat A.I. as a first opinion and confirm with a human professional. Trust, but verify.

4. Criminals have A.I. too

The same technology can copy a voice from a 10-second clip. That "grandchild in trouble" call may be a computer wearing your grandchild's voice. This is why the family code word from the scam lesson matters more every year. A.I. can fake a voice; it cannot fake your family's secret word.

5. You are not too late

Here is the secret nobody says out loud: everyone is a beginner at A.I. — including the 30-year-olds. This technology is only a few years old. For the first time in your life, you and your grandchildren are starting a technology at the same starting line. Type one question into ChatGPT this week and you'll be ahead of half the country.
