BONUS SECRET

"How To Legally Print Money On The Internet"

No, this is not about cryptocurrency, day trading, or anything with the word "guru" in it. "Printing money on the internet" means something much more boring and much more real: the internet pays for things you already own and things you already know.

Money printer #1 — The treasure in your closets

The average American home contains thousands of dollars of things nobody uses: tools, china, furniture, records, jewelry, that exercise bike. Facebook Marketplace and eBay are garage sales with millions of customers.

The recipe:
1. Take three photos in good light (you learned the camera in Lesson 2).
2. Search the app for the same item to see what it sells for.
3. Write two honest sentences and your price.
4. Meet buyers in a public place — many police stations offer free "safe exchange" parking spots — or ship with the label the app makes for you.

Most people's first month of decluttering pays a few hundred dollars. Some closets pay a lot more.

Money printer #2 — Forty years of knowing things

You spent a lifetime learning something — bookkeeping, sewing, engine repair, piano, perfect pie crust. The internet is full of people who will pay to learn it. Grandparents tutor math over Zoom (Lesson 4!) for $30–60 an hour. Retired tradesmen answer questions by video call. Quilters sell patterns. Your experience is inventory that never runs out.

The safety rules (read twice)

1. Never accept a check for more than your price ("just refund the difference") — that's the oldest scam online.
2. Gift cards are never payment. Cash in person, or the app's own payment button.
3. Never give your bank login to "verify" anything.
4. If a buyer is in a huge hurry — remember the golden rule from Lesson 3. Urgency is the fingerprint of fraud.

The honest math

Will this replace a pension? No — anyone who promises that is selling something. Will an unhurried hour a day cover the grocery bill, the grandkids' birthdays, or a vacation fund? For thousands of retirees, yes. That's real money, printed legally, from your kitchen table.

And notice something: every skill this lesson used — camera, texting, avoiding scams, Zoom — came from earlier lessons. That's what tech mastery is for. It was never about the phone. It was about what the phone makes possible.

Congratulations — you've completed the NO B.S. Guide.
