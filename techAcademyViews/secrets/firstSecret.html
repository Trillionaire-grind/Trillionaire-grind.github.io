Welcome. What you are about to read is the single most important lesson in this entire academy. Every other skill — the phone, the computer, Zoom, even A.I. — sits on top of this one idea.

The secret, in one sentence

You cannot break your phone or computer by tapping the wrong thing.

That's it. That is the secret that separates people who are "good with technology" from people who feel left behind.

Think about the last time you watched a child use a phone. They tap everything. They swipe in every direction. They open apps they have never seen before. Nobody taught them — they simply are not afraid. And because they are not afraid, they learn in minutes what takes a fearful adult months.

Here is what they know that nobody ever told you: modern technology is built to be explored. Billions of dollars were spent making these devices forgiving. The wrong tap never breaks anything. The worst thing that can happen is that you end up on a screen you don't recognize — and there is always a way back.

The three rules of tech mastery

Rule 1 — Tap boldly. If you tap something and don't like what happened, you can always close it and start over. On an iPhone, swipe up from the bottom (or press the round home button). On Android, tap the circle at the bottom. That one move rescues you from any screen, every time. Practice it until it is automatic — it is your escape hatch.

Rule 2 — Slow is fine. The screen will wait for you all day. There is no timer. Nobody is watching. Read every screen fully before you tap. People who rush make mistakes; people who read every word never get lost.

Rule 3 — Everything is a recipe. Every tech skill — sending a photo, joining a Zoom call, paying a bill — is just a list of steps done in the same order every time, exactly like a recipe. You already follow recipes. Do the steps enough times and your fingers remember them without thinking, the same way you dial a phone number you know by heart.

Your 15-minute habit

Mastery does not come from a marathon. It comes from 15 minutes a day of fearless practice. Pick one small thing — today, practice your escape hatch (Rule 1) ten times. Open any app, then go home. Open another, go home. By tomorrow it will be automatic, and a device that can always be rescued is a device you can never fear.

Do this daily and in 30 days you will be the one your friends call for help.
